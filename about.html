About

A community-built record of what Australian homes are really like.

Open House started with a straightforward observation: when you're choosing where to live, the property listing tells you about the polished floors and the views. It rarely tells you about the broken lift, the mould in winter, or the cafe down the road that quietly becomes the best part of your week.

Why we built this

Most people have lived somewhere they wish they'd known more about before signing. Most people have also lived in a place that turned out to be a quiet gem.

Open House is built to capture that knowledge in one place. The more reviews we collect, the better-informed every choice that follows becomes. The idea is simple, but it only works if enough people contribute.

Whether you're renting, buying for the first time, or weighing up an investment, Open House gives you a fuller picture of an address before you commit to it.

How it works

1 · Search an address

Type in any property in Australia. If we have reviews on the exact dwelling, we'll show them. If we don't, we'll show what we know about the building, the street, and the surrounding suburb, alongside a map of nearby reviewed properties.

2 · Read the summary, dig deeper if you want

Each property page leads with an AI-generated summary that synthesises every resident review. Below that, you'll find the underlying ratings and a per-reviewer breakdown, also AI-generated from each reviewer's slider scores and notes. You decide how deeply to read.

3 · Contribute a review

The review form is a series of sliders covering the home, the building (where applicable), and the suburb. Most people complete it in around four minutes. Your name and exact unit number stay private. We publish a first name and initial only.

4 · Verification keeps reviews honest

Submitting a review requires uploading one piece of proof you lived at the address. A redacted utility bill, lease, or addressed mail. Verified reviews get a green tick on the public page and weight more heavily in the AI summary.

What we deliberately leave out

Open House reviews the home, the building and the surrounding area. We don't publish reviews of specific landlords, real estate agents, individual neighbours or strata managers. Those reviews are difficult to verify, easy to misuse, and rarely useful to the next reader.

We also don't publish interior photos, exact unit numbers, or anything that could identify the reviewer or the current resident.

Community charter

Open House works because of the people who contribute to it. A short list of expectations:

  • Be specific. "Bad" doesn't help anyone. "The lift broke down six times in two years" does.
  • Be fair. Review the place, not the people. No naming individual neighbours, agents or owners.
  • Be honest. If something was great, say so. If it was a problem, say so. Reviews exist for the next reader, not for the venting.
  • Be respectful. Read your review back as if you were describing the address to a friend.

Common questions

Is leaving a review anonymous?

Mostly. We publish only your first name and last initial. Your unit number and verification documents are never shown publicly.

What if my old place isn't listed yet?

Almost every property on Open House started with a single resident deciding to share what they knew. You'd be the first.

Can a landlord or agent take down a bad review?

No, but they can flag a review for moderation if they believe it's false or breaches our charter. We assess flagged reviews against the original verification documents.

Is Open House free?

Yes, for residents and people researching addresses. Always. We're considering a paid tier for industry users (agents, investors, property managers) but resident reviews and searches will always remain free.

Ready to contribute?

The site only works because residents share what they know. Spend four minutes on an address you've lived at. Your insights help someone you'll never meet.
