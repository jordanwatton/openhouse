About

A community record of what Australian homes are really like.

The listing tells you about the floors and the views. It rarely tells you about the broken lift, the mould in winter, or the cafe down the road that quietly becomes the best part of your week. Open House is that second story.

Why we built this

Two stories about every address.

Most people have lived somewhere they wish they'd known more about before signing. Most people have also lived in a place that turned out to be a quiet gem.

Open House captures both. The more residents contribute, the better-informed every choice that follows becomes. The idea's simple — it only works if enough people do it.

How it works

Four steps. No tricks.

01 / Search

Type any Australian address.

If anyone's reviewed it, you'll see the home, the building and the surrounding suburb. If not, we show what we know from public sources and a map of nearby reviewed places.

02 / Read

Get the summary first, dig deeper if you want.

Every property page leads with an AI summary that synthesises the verified reviews. Below that, rating breakdowns and individual reviews. You decide how deep to go.

03 / Contribute

Five minutes, mostly sliders.

The review form is mostly sliders plus a few short prose answers. Cover the home, the building (if it's an apartment), and the suburb.

04 / Verify

Proof of residence keeps things honest.

One redacted utility bill, lease or addressed mail is enough. Verified reviews carry a tick and count more in the summary. Your documents are never shown publicly.

What we leave out

The home, the building, the suburb. Nothing else.

Open House reviews the home, the building and the surrounding area. We don't publish reviews of specific landlords, real estate agents, individual neighbours or strata managers.

Those reviews are difficult to verify, easy to misuse, and rarely useful to the next reader. We also don't publish interior photos, exact unit numbers, or anything that could identify a reviewer or current resident.

Specifically, no reviews of:

  • Individual landlords or owners
  • Real estate agents
  • Strata managers or building managers
  • Specific neighbours
  • Tenants or short-term residents

Community charter

What we ask of you.

01

Be specific.

"Bad" doesn't help anyone. "The lift broke down six times in two years" does.

02

Be fair.

Review the place, not the people. No naming individual neighbours, agents or owners.

03

Be honest.

If something was great, say so. If it was a problem, say so. Reviews exist for the next reader, not for venting.

04

Be respectful.

Read it back as if you were describing the address to a friend.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

Is leaving a review anonymous?

Mostly. We publish only your first name and last initial. Your unit number and verification documents are never shown publicly.

What if my old place isn't listed yet?

Almost every property on Open House started with a single resident sharing what they knew. You'd be the first — and the address stays on the site forever after that.

Can a landlord or agent take down a bad review?

No, but they can flag a review for moderation if they believe it's false or breaches our charter. We assess flagged reviews against the original verification documents.

Is Open House free?

Yes — for residents and people researching addresses, always. We're considering a paid tier for industry users (agents, investors, property managers), but resident reviews and searches will always remain free.
